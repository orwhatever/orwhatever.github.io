Texture Mapping

Sorry; your web browser does not support HTML5’s canvas element.

Using a simple point sampling, the texture appears with nice contrast, however aliasing is happening further into the horizon. By using linear sampling, the image appears smoother in the middle depths, but image appears blurry in the closer depths.

Using mipmapping with point sampling, gives a nice visual result. The contrast is nice in the closer depths and gradually becomes blurry as the depth increases. There is however color averaging deep in the horizon due to the mip mapping. Combining linear mapping with mipmapping gives a smoother transition between usages of different mipmapping levels in the cost of higher color averaging. In conclusion, the best visual effect is point sampling with mipmapping, where a nice compromise is done between contrast levels and color averaging.
